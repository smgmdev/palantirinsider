Kleos Space
Simplified Analysis

Space-powered RF/SIGINT company. What's good, what's not, and how it stacks up against Palantir and Project MAX.
01
Pros & Cons

Kleos collects radio frequency signals from space — detecting any RF transmitter on land or sea. Real contracts with NRO (US), GCHQ (UK), and DRM (France) prove the tech works. Here's the honest breakdown.

✓  What's Good
Works in any weatherRF signals pass through clouds, smoke, night. Optical satellites go blind — Kleos doesn't.
Catches hidden targetsShips with AIS off, troops under cover, jammers — all emit RF. You can't hide your radio.
NRO + GCHQ contractsThe hardest intelligence agencies in the world to sell to. Proves operational capability, not just demos.
Patent-pending geolocationUnder 250m precision using 4 satellites. Proprietary TDOA/FDOA Doppler algorithm. Hard to replicate.
EU sovereign — no US controlLuxembourg HQ. Nations blocked from US platforms (Palantir) can use Kleos freely.
Multiplies other sensors"Tipping imagery" — RF pinpoints a target to 250m, then tasks optical satellites exactly there. 10× more efficient.
General Atomics integrationAlready embedded in MQ-9 Reaper operator platform. Live military pipeline proven.
✗  What's Weak
Only 12 satellitesPlanet has 200+. ICEYE has 35+. Kleos has 12 — with the first cluster dying mid-2024. Coverage gaps are significant.
RF only — no visualsDetects a signal exists. Can't tell you if it's a fishing boat or a warship. No EO, no SAR, no IR whatsoever.
Zero AI layerDelivers GPS coordinates and signal data. No object classification, no threat scoring, no pattern-of-life. Just dots on a map.
Can't detect missiles or dronesA ballistic missile has no radio transmitter. Kleos is completely blind to the core threat detection mission.
Tiny contracts — €300k–€500k/zonePalantir charges $1.3B per contract. Kleos' entire global market is $0.5B/yr. No path to scale.
Hardware dying — no replacement announcedAll 3 clusters EOL by 2027. No next-gen constellation publicly announced. Existential risk within 4 years.
No platform — just a data viewerLOCATE OPTIX is a basic web map. Not a decision platform. No analyst workflows, no kill-chain, no automation.

04
Final Verdict

Kleos in 3 Sentences

Kleos built something technically real — space-based RF geolocation that NRO and GCHQ actually pay for. But their hardware is dying, their TAM is tiny, and they have no AI, no platform, and no path to scale as a standalone company.

They are not a competitor to Palantir or Project MAX. They are a specialist data ingredient — valuable as a layer inside a bigger platform, worthless as a standalone defence company.
