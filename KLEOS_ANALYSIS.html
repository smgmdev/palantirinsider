Section 01
What Kleos Actually Is

Kleos Space is a space-based RF/SIGINT data company headquartered in Luxembourg, with offices in Leicester (UK) and Denver (USA). They operate small satellite clusters equipped with Radio Frequency sensors that passively detect and geolocate any RF transmitter on land or sea — military radios, ship communications, drone control signals, jamming equipment.

They collect the raw RF spectrum, process it through their proprietary ground-based platform, and sell the resulting location intelligence as a subscription data product to governments and analytics companies.

Kleos is not an AI company. It is not a surveillance platform. It is a data collection company that sells signal intelligence — dots on a map with metadata — to customers who then build their own analysis on top.

Core Technology Stack

Sensor Layer
RF Spectrum Collection (VHF + X-Band)
Satellites passively listen to radio frequencies. Collect raw IQ signal data across VHF (maritime/military comms) and X-Band. No active radar, no optical camera, no infrared — purely passive RF listening.
Processing Layer
TDOA / FDOA Geolocation Algorithm
Time Difference of Arrival + Frequency Difference of Arrival + Doppler Rate processing across 4 satellites per cluster. Achieves <250m position accuracy at 95% confidence with 4 satellites. Patent pending.
Delivery Layer
LOCATE Product Suite + OPTIX Visualiser
LOCATE (geospatial coords), LOCATE+ (raw IQ files), LOCATE X (signal-enriched), LOCATE OPTIX (web visualiser). Delivered via API or the OPTIX platform. Basic intelligence visualisation tool — not a decision platform.

Pricing

Individual subscriptions for a VHF daily visit to one Standard Collection Zone (SCZ) range from €300,000 – €500,000 per year (A$500k–A$800k). Broader distribution rights negotiated per customer. Mission-as-a-Service contracts priced per coverage percentage, taskability level, and exclusivity.

Context: €300k–€500k per zone per year. Palantir's Maven Smart System contract is $1.3B for essentially the same end mission. Kleos is selling the raw ingredient; Palantir is selling the cooked meal at 2,000× markup.
Section 02
What's Genuinely Valuable

Despite being a small company, Kleos has built something technically real and operationally proven. These are the things worth respecting — and potentially acquiring.

Genuine Moat
RF Geolocation from Space — Unique Capability
There is no commercial equivalent at this price point and scale. Space-based SIGINT was previously only available to NSA/GCHQ. Kleos democratised it. The TDOA/FDOA patent-pending algorithm is proprietary and years ahead of most competitors in commercial SIGINT.
All-Weather Advantage
Works Where EO Satellites Go Blind
Cloud cover, smoke, night, jungle canopy — none of it affects RF collection. Electro-optical satellites (Planet, Maxar, BlackSky) are useless in bad weather. Kleos keeps working 24/7 regardless. In conflict zones like Ukraine, where smoke and overcast are constant, this is mission-critical.
Deception Detection
Catches Targets That Are Actively Hiding
Ships turning off AIS transponders, troops under tree cover, vehicles with no visual signature — Kleos detects them all because they can't turn off their radios. AIS spoofing is rampant in conflict zones. This is the only commercial solution that bypasses it completely.
Tier 1 Validation
NRO + GCHQ + DRM Contracts
The NRO is the most demanding satellite intelligence customer on Earth. GCHQ is the UK equivalent of NSA. DRM is French military intelligence. All three buying from a 12-satellite startup proves the technology genuinely works at the operational level — not just in demos.
Force Multiplier
"Tipping Imagery" — Makes Other Sensors 10× More Effective
Kleos RF signals cue optical satellites exactly where to point. Instead of scanning thousands of km², the analyst knows a target is within 250m — then tasks an EO or SAR satellite to confirm visually. This multiplies the value of every other sensor in the stack dramatically.
EU Sovereign
Luxembourg HQ — Non-US Platform
Like Project MAX, Kleos is not subject to US ITAR/EAR export controls in the same way as Palantir or Planet. Nations that cannot or will not use US platforms can use Kleos. This is a growing market as data sovereignty concerns spread globally.
Integration Ready
Already Embedded in General Atomics (MQ-9)
General Atomics — the manufacturer of the MQ-9 Reaper drone — already integrates Kleos data in their GEOINT/SIGINT/COMINT demo platform. This means the data pipeline into real military operations is already proven and operational.
Blind Spot Filler
The One Sensor Type Palantir Can't Easily License
Palantir licenses EO (Planet, Maxar), SAR (ICEYE), weather (Tomorrow.io) — but has no deep space-based SIGINT partner at scale. Kleos fills this specific gap. Acquiring Kleos would give Palantir — or Project MAX — a capability its competitor does not have.
Bottom line on value: Kleos has a real, defensible, operationally proven technology that solves a genuine problem no one else solves commercially. The NRO/GCHQ contracts are the strongest possible third-party validation. The RF geolocation moat is real.
Section 03
What's Weak / Worthless vs Palantir
Scale Problem
Only 12 Satellites — Palantir Partners Have 200+
Planet Labs (200 sats), ICEYE (35 sats), Maxar, BlackSky — all licensed by Palantir simultaneously. Kleos has 12 satellites, with KSF-1 dying mid-2024. Their revisit rate per zone is measured in hours, not minutes. For persistent surveillance, this is inadequate.
Tiny Market
$0.3B–$0.5B TAM — Palantir's Single Contract Is Bigger
Kleos' own presentation claims the total global market for RF data is $300M–$500M/year. Palantir's Maven Smart System contract alone is $1.3B. Kleos' addressable market is literally smaller than one Palantir deal. There is no path to $1B revenue on RF data subscriptions alone.
No AI
Zero Intelligence Layer — Delivers Dots, Not Decisions
Kleos tells you a 25W radio transmitter is at these coordinates. That's it. No object classification, no threat scoring, no pattern-of-life analysis, no anomaly detection, no fusion with other sensors. Palantir takes that dot and tells you it's a Russian T-72 that moved 3km in the last hour and is 4km from a friendly position.
Single Sensor
RF Only — No Visual, No Thermal, No SAR
RF detects presence. It cannot identify what's there. A signal at 156.5MHz tells you a maritime radio is transmitting — it doesn't tell you if it's a fishing boat or a warship. Without EO or SAR fusion, you have an incomplete picture. Palantir fuses all sensor types simultaneously.
No Threat Detection
Cannot Detect Missiles, Drones, Nuclear Activity
The core Project MAX use case — detecting ballistic missile launches, drone swarms, CBRN signatures — requires IR satellites, not RF. Kleos is entirely blind to a missile in flight. A hypersonic missile has no radio transmitter. This is a fundamental capability gap vs the mission.
Tiny Contracts
€300k–€500k Per Zone — No Path to Large Deals
At €400k average per zone subscription, Kleos needs 2,500 zone subscriptions to hit €1B revenue. That's commercially impossible. Their pricing model was designed for a €50M ARR business, not a platform company. The revenue architecture cannot support scale without a complete model redesign.
Ageing Hardware
All 3 Clusters Reach EOL by 2027 — No Next-Gen Announced
KSF-1: EOL mid-2024. KSF-2: EOL 2026. KSF-3: EOL 2027. Within 4 years of this document, Kleos has no satellites. Their revenue model slide was labelled DRAFT. There is no publicly announced next-generation constellation. This is an existential risk.
No Platform
LOCATE OPTIX Is a Map Tool, Not a Decision System
Palantir's AIP has object detection, change detection, sensor fusion, an ontology knowledge graph, analyst workflows, and kill-chain integration. Kleos' LOCATE OPTIX is a web map where you can click dots and see signal spectrograms. There is no comparison. It is a data viewer, not a platform.
No Autonomous Response
Data Only — No Action Layer
Kleos detects. It does not respond. There is no connection to any effector — no drone tasking, no intercept routing, no alert pipeline. An analyst must manually review the data and decide what to do. In a real-time threat scenario, this human latency is unacceptable.
Structural problem: Kleos is a data ingredient company trying to survive in a market where the platforms (Palantir, Project MAX) capture 90% of the value. Their entire business is the cost of goods sold in someone else's P&L.

Where Kleos Beats Palantir

RF/SIGINT Superiority
Space-Based Signal Intelligence
Palantir has no native SIGINT capability. They license from HawkEye 360 and similar — but Kleos' geolocation precision (<250m) and VHF+X-Band dual coverage is ahead of what Palantir's current SIGINT partners offer.
Data Sovereignty
EU-Sovereign, No US Oversight
Palantir is a US company. Its data flows through US-controlled infrastructure. For EU member states and non-Five-Eyes nations, Kleos is accessible in ways Palantir is not. NRO + GCHQ using both says a lot.
Deception Resistance
Detects What Palantir's Sources Miss
When AIS is spoofed, when optical is clouded out, when SAR is tasked elsewhere — Kleos still sees RF emitters. It uniquely plugs the gap when all of Palantir's licensed sensors are compromised simultaneously.

Section 06
Strategic Verdict & Project MAX Recommendation

Kleos is not a Palantir competitor. They are not even in the same category. Palantir is the AI brain that processes intelligence. Kleos is one type of sensor that feeds raw data into that brain. They operate at completely different layers of the stack — and Kleos knows it. Their integration with BlackSky and General Atomics shows they understand their role as a data supplier, not a platform.

The Three-Sentence Verdict

What Kleos is: The best commercial space-based SIGINT data company in the world — with real NRO/GCHQ contracts to prove it. A specialist ingredient that fills a genuine gap no EO or SAR satellite can fill. A small company with an ageing constellation, a tiny TAM, no AI layer, and no path to scale on its own.
What Kleos isn't: A Palantir competitor. A platform. A company with a path to $1B revenue as a standalone. Their revenue model slide had a DRAFT watermark as of May 2023. They are a niche data vendor selling the raw material that Palantir packages into billion-dollar contracts.
What Kleos is worth to Project MAX: Acquiring Kleos for $50M–$150M adds the one sensor type Project MAX's own constellation doesn't natively have — space-based SIGINT. It fills Palantir's blind spot (hidden vessels, AIS spoofing, RF emitters) and gives Project MAX a sensor Palantir has to license from third parties. At that price it's a rounding error in the build budget.

Final Assessment

Kleos is a well-executed niche company that solved a hard technical problem and got tier-1 intelligence agencies as customers. That is genuinely impressive. But their ceiling is low, their hardware is dying, and their business model can't scale. They are a bolt-on acquisition target — not a competitor, not a threat, and not a platform.

Palantir's advantage over Kleos isn't technology. It's the AI layer, the government relationships, the platform lock-in, and the contract scale. Project MAX beats both because it owns the full stack — sensors, AI, response — and builds 15-year sovereign contracts instead of €400k annual subscriptions.
