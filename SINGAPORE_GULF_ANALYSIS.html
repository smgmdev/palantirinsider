Entry Strategy: How to Win These Markets

Common patterns and differentiation levers across the Singapore + Gulf corridor.

01 — Sovereignty First
Every country here has a sovereignty constraint. Deploy on their national cloud (Oracle for Singapore, Azure for Kuwait/Qatar, AWS/Azure for Saudi). Palantir's strength is also its weakness: US-origin triggers data sovereignty concerns. Position as "sovereign-first, US-independent."
02 — Build On, Not Against
G42 in UAE, HUMAIN in Saudi, Qai in Qatar, Microsoft in Kuwait — these are entrenched. Don't compete directly. Position as the intelligence layer that makes their existing investments more powerful. Interoperability is the product.
03 — Sector Wedges
Enter via a high-value sector, not a broad platform pitch: maritime AI for Bahrain, oil infrastructure AI for Kuwait, joint ops AI for Singapore. Prove value in one silo, then expand to full Foundry-equivalent. Sector wedges close 10x faster than platform deals.
04 — Talent Pipeline
All six countries have national AI ambitions but thin talent pools. An "AI bootcamp" offer (like Palantir's AIP Bootcamps) creates lock-in, builds local advocacy, and is genuinely valued. Saudi HUMAIN and Singapore DIS both need this.
